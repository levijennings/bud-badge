## 3. Cannabis Laws Are Complex and Vary Significantly

**Cannabis regulations are governed at multiple legal levels and vary dramatically by jurisdiction:**

### Federal Level
- Cannabis remains a Schedule I controlled substance under the Controlled Substances Act (21 U.S.C. § 812)
- Federal law prohibits cultivation, distribution, and possession of cannabis regardless of state law
- Federal prosecutions are possible even in states where cannabis is legal
- Federal banking, tax, and employment regulations create complex compliance issues

### State Level
- 38+ states have legalized cannabis for medical use
- 24+ states have legalized cannabis for adult recreational use
- Each state has distinct regulatory frameworks, including:
  - Licensing requirements and procedures
  - Cultivation limits and growing requirements
  - Retail and distribution regulations
  - Testing and quality standards
  - Employee training and certification requirements
  - Background check requirements
  - Record-keeping and reporting obligations

### Local and Municipal Level
- Individual counties, cities, and municipalities may prohibit cannabis operations entirely
- Local regulations often exceed state requirements
- Zoning and location restrictions apply in many jurisdictions
- Licensing may require local approval in addition to state approval

### Industry-Specific Regulations
- Cannabis businesses face unique employment law requirements
- Workplace testing and accommodation obligations vary
- Licensing requirements for managers, handlers, and employees differ by state
- Financial and tax compliance requirements are complex due to federal prohibition

## 8. Specific Compliance Areas

### Employment and Workplace
- **Background Checks:** Many jurisdictions require specific background checks for cannabis employees; requirements vary significantly
- **Testing:** Workplace cannabis testing laws vary; some states restrict testing while others require it
- **Accommodation:** Some states protect medical cannabis use by employees; others do not
- **Training Requirements:** States vary on mandated training content and frequency
- **Record-Keeping:** Requirements for employee training records differ by jurisdiction

### Licensing and Operations
- **Applicant Requirements:** Eligibility, background checks, and ownership restrictions vary
- **Operational Conditions:** License conditions address cultivation limits, retail hours, track-and-trace requirements, etc.
- **Compliance Monitoring:** Regulatory agencies conduct inspections and investigations
- **Violation Penalties:** Civil and criminal penalties apply for violations

### Financial and Tax
- **Banking:** Federal prohibition prevents normal banking relationships
- **Tax Implications:** IRC Section 280E denies cannabis business expense deductions
- **Financial Reporting:** Complex reporting requirements apply
- **Money Laundering:** Anti-money laundering laws apply despite federal prohibition
