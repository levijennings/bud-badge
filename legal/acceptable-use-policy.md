# Acceptable Use Policy

**Last Updated:** April 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") outlines the prohibited activities and conduct standards for users of Bud Badge (operated by DVLMNT). By using Bud Badge, you agree to comply with this policy.

## 1. Prohibited Activities

You agree not to use Bud Badge in any manner that:

### 1.1 Illegal Activities
- Violates any applicable federal, state, local, or international law or regulation
- Violates cannabis laws or regulations in your jurisdiction
- Involves fraud, embezzlement, money laundering, or financial crimes
- Constitutes harassment, stalking, discrimination, or abuse
- Involves threats, violence, or harm to others
- Involves misrepresentation to regulatory authorities
- Infringes intellectual property rights
- Violates privacy or employment laws

### 1.2 System Interference
- Attempts unauthorized access to Bud Badge or its systems
- Disrupts the normal operation of the Service
- Overloads servers with excessive requests (DDoS attacks)
- Exploits vulnerabilities or bugs
- Interferes with network connectivity or functionality
- Transmits malware, viruses, or harmful code
- Uses automated tools to scrape or harvest data without authorization
- Reverse-engineers or attempts to extract source code

### 1.3 Fraudulent or Deceptive Conduct
- Misrepresents organizational information or employee status
- Falsifies training completion records or certifications
- Creates fake accounts or impersonates others
- Engages in phishing or social engineering
- Falsifies employee certifications or training data
- Misrepresents credentials to regulatory authorities
- Manipulates assessment results or scores

### 1.4 Spam and Abuse
- Sends unsolicited commercial emails or communications
- Posts repetitive, irrelevant, or off-topic content
- Exploits the messaging or support system
- Creates content designed to manipulate algorithms

### 1.5 Harmful Content
- Posts content containing hate speech, discrimination, or racism
- Promotes violence, self-harm, or illegal activities
- Shares sexually explicit or obscene content
- Discloses others' private information without consent
- Posts defamatory or false statements about individuals or organizations
- Harasses, bullies, or threatens other users
- Encourages violation of laws or regulations

### 1.6 Intellectual Property Violations
- Uploads or shares copyrighted content without authorization
- Violates trademark rights
- Shares trade secrets or confidential information
- Uses training materials beyond licensed scope

## 2. Product-Specific Rules

### 2.1 Employer Responsibilities

Employers agree to:
- Ensure employees understand that Bud Badge is a training tool, not a compliance guarantee
- Maintain accurate account information
- Manage employee access and accounts appropriately
- Maintain security of account credentials
- Comply with all applicable cannabis and employment laws
- Monitor employee compliance with training assignments
- Not falsify or manipulate training records

Employers agree NOT to:
- Falsify employee training records or completion dates
- Allow employees to complete training under false identities
- Claim certifications for uncompleted training
- Manipulate assessment results or bypass assessments
- Misrepresent training completion to regulatory authorities
- Use training records to make discriminatory employment decisions
- Share employee training data with unauthorized third parties
- Falsify organizational information or credentials

### 2.2 Employee Responsibilities

Employees agree to:
- Complete assigned training modules honestly and thoroughly
- Provide accurate information in training
- Not share account credentials with others
- Understand that training completion does not guarantee regulatory compliance
- Comply with training content and organizational policies

Employees agree NOT to:
- Allow others to complete training using their account
- Falsify training information or assessment answers
- Claim certificates for training not completed
- Share training content outside their organization
- Bypass or circumvent training modules or assessments

### 2.3 Training Data Accuracy

You agree to:
- Ensure training content accurately reflects your jurisdiction's requirements
- Supplement Bud Badge training with jurisdiction-specific information
- Consult with legal counsel about specific compliance obligations
- Update training when applicable laws change
- Not misrepresent training as providing legal compliance guarantees

You agree NOT to:
- Rely solely on Bud Badge for regulatory compliance
- Claim that Bud Badge training guarantees regulatory compliance
- Misrepresent training effectiveness or completeness to regulators
- Use training to circumvent regulatory requirements

### 2.4 Certification and Records

You agree to:
- Understand that certifications indicate training completion, not legal compliance
- Maintain accurate records of training completion
- Provide records to regulatory agencies upon request
- Retain records for the period required by law
- Not alter, falsify, or destroy records

You agree NOT to:
- Present certifications as government licenses or official approvals
- Create false certifications or backdated certificates
- Alter training records or completion dates
- Use certifications to mislead employees or regulators
- Destroy or delete records required by law

### 2.5 Regulatory Compliance Claims

You agree to:
- Understand that Bud Badge provides training tools, not legal advice
- Consult with legal counsel for compliance questions
- Not rely solely on Bud Badge for regulatory compliance
- Maintain responsibility for actual regulatory compliance
- Respond honestly to regulatory inquiries

You agree NOT to:
- Claim that using Bud Badge guarantees regulatory compliance
- Represent Bud Badge certifications as official regulatory credentials
- Use Bud Badge training to circumvent legal requirements
- Misrepresent training effectiveness or compliance outcomes
- Mislead regulators about your compliance measures

## 3. Content Standards

All user-generated content must:

3.1 **Be Accurate:** Content must not contain false or misleading information, especially regarding cannabis regulations or compliance status.

3.2 **Be Legal:** Content must not promote illegal activities or encourage violation of laws.

3.3 **Be Truthful:** Content related to training, certifications, or compliance must be honest and accurate.

3.4 **Protect Privacy:** Content must not disclose private information about individuals without consent.

3.5 **Respect IP:** Content must not infringe the intellectual property rights of others.

## 4. Enforcement and Consequences

### 4.1 Monitoring
DVLMNT monitors user activity for compliance with this AUP, including:
- Training completion patterns and anomalies
- Record falsification attempts
- Account misuse and circumvention
- Unauthorized data access or sharing

### 4.2 Content Removal
We may remove or suspend content that violates this AUP, including:
- False or manipulated training records
- Violative communications
- Spam or abuse
- Content promoting illegal activity

### 4.3 Account Warnings
For minor violations, we may issue a warning describing the violation and requesting corrective action within a specified timeframe.

### 4.4 Account Suspension
For serious or repeated violations, we may temporarily suspend your account, restricting:
- Access to the Service
- Ability to create new training assignments
- Data access and management capabilities
- Employee management functions

### 4.5 Account Termination
We may permanently terminate your account if you:
- Repeatedly violate this AUP despite warnings
- Commit serious violations (fraud, falsification of records, illegal activity)
- Attempt to circumvent suspension restrictions
- Falsify training records or certifications
- Pose a threat to the Service or regulatory compliance of others

### 4.6 Regulatory Reporting
For serious violations, including record falsification or fraud, we may:
- Report the matter to relevant regulatory authorities
- Cooperate with regulatory investigations
- Disclose account information as required by law
- Preserve evidence for potential legal proceedings

### 4.7 Legal Action
We may pursue civil or criminal legal action for:
- Fraud or financial crimes
- Record falsification
- Regulatory violations
- Threats or harm to others

## 5. Reporting Violations

5.1 If you observe a violation of this AUP, please report it by:
- Emailing levi@dvlmnt.com with "AUP Violation Report" in the subject line

5.2 Your report should include:
- Description of the violation
- Reference to the violating account or content
- Your contact information
- Supporting evidence if available

5.3 We will investigate reports promptly and take appropriate action.

5.4 False or bad-faith reports may result in warnings or account suspension.

## 6. Appeals

6.1 If your account has been suspended or terminated, you may appeal within 30 days by emailing levi@dvlmnt.com with "Account Appeal" in the subject line.

6.2 Your appeal should include:
- Explanation of why you believe the action was inappropriate
- Evidence supporting your position
- Commitment to future compliance

6.3 We will review appeals within 14 days and notify you of the outcome.

## 7. Changes to This Policy

DVLMNT reserves the right to modify this AUP at any time. Changes are effective upon posting. Your continued use constitutes acceptance of modifications.

## 8. Miscellaneous

8.1 **Legal Compliance:** Regardless of this AUP, you agree to comply with all applicable laws, including cannabis regulations in your jurisdiction.

8.2 **Regulatory Cooperation:** You agree to cooperate with regulatory inquiries and provide records as required by law.

8.3 **Severability:** If any provision is unenforceable, remaining provisions continue in effect.

8.4 **No Waiver:** Failure to enforce this AUP in specific instances does not constitute a waiver of DVLMNT's rights.
